Trip to Nigeria

Travel

I decided to travel to nigeria to get a better view of how a post-colonial country is doing.

I took a plane from Gardemoen and landed in the capital Abuja the trip itself cost 8241 kr and it took a little over 10 hours.

When I arrived in Abuja is quite late so I decided to take a taxi to the hotel I was staying at Angel Park place Hotel it cost 458 kr per night and had a nice cozy room.

Museum Trip

The next day I decided to visit the national museum to get a better view of the history of Nigeria pre-colonialism, but it was in Lagos so it took a while to travel there.

I found out that Nigeria was mostly a hunter gatherer community with many kingdoms and many languages.

I decided to ask one of the workers why Britain was interested in Nigeria in the first place and they said that Britain was mostly interested for the resources.

After the trip to the museum I deccided to get some food at a local restaurant where i ordered some Jollof which is the national dish in Nigeria it consists mostly of rice and spices.

Traveling Around

The next few days I decided to just travel around the city and just looking around and taking in the country.

I went around some official builidngs and was surprised to see that most of the signs was in english so I asked a bit around and found out that english was the national language in Nigeria.

I also found out through the many times I had talked to Nigerians that they have their own dialect of English where they use some different words and fraces.

this caught me offguard many times due to my unfamiliarity with it.

I even visited a football stadium though there were no games at the moment. during my trip to the stadium I also learned that football is very popular.

I checked out the national assembly building where they handle political matters

I also found out they still have English law.

From what i could tell Nigeria still has good relations with Britain and the closest controversy/problem would be the biafra rebellion
